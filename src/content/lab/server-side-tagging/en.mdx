Client-side tracking has been losing ground for years: ad-blockers, ITP, short cookie lifetimes and consent rejection mean a growing share of events **never reaches reporting**. The gap isn't uniform — it grows over time and skews every trend.

## Where the gap appears

Rather than asserting this in prose, you can show it. The mini-dashboard below is embedded right here — switch the view and hover the curve. This is how data stories should work: **explain by letting people interact.**

<ConversionsChart />

> Server-side doesn't capture more people — it loses less of those who already consent.

## What changes

Server-side tagging moves capture into a controlled environment: a single clean request to your own server container that decides what flows where — enriched, filtered, or dropped. Data quality rises, third-party scripts in the browser drop, consent stays enforceable.

The point is not "more tracking at any cost" but **less data loss at the same consent** — and therefore more dependable trends to base decisions on.
