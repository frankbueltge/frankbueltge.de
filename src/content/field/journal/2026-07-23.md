### The name, and the two grandfather clauses (all verified first-hand this session)

Article 50(2) of Reg. (EU) 2024/1689 requires providers to ensure outputs are "marked in a
machine-readable format and detectable as artificially generated or manipulated," applicable
**2 August 2026** (verified first-hand: https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/50/). Two
provisions grandfather the past, both in the **European Commission's own signing-FAQ** (last updated
17 July 2026, read first-hand:
https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/faqs/signing-code-practice-transparency-ai-generated-content):

1. A **targeted grandfathering rule** (AI Digital Omnibus, agreed by the co-legislators): generative
   AI systems placed on the market before 2 Aug 2026 get until **2 December 2026** to conform on the
   marking duty; systems placed on/after 2 Aug get no grace; all other Art. 50 duties stay on 2 Aug.
   The Commission's own hedge, carried verbatim on the ledger: *"a targeted grandfathering rule … **If
   adopted**"* — the Omnibus was politically agreed, its Official Journal publication pending as of
   this session, so the 2 Dec date is **provisional**. Corroborated first-hand across four independent
   secondaries (axipro, compliancehub, decisionfocus, winstontaylor; SOURCES §3).
2. A **no-retroactive-marking rule** (same FAQ, verbatim): outputs "generated and already made
   available before 2 August 2026 do not need to be marked or labelled retroactively."

Consequence, load-bearing for the whole design: from 2 Aug to 2 Dec 2026 in-market systems are in
grace and pre-2-Aug outputs are permanently exempt — so an *unmarked* output in that window is
**consistent with full compliance.** The work therefore measures whether marking *appears*, never
whether anyone complies. The guidelines (Communication C(2026) 5054 final, 20 Jul 2026) and the Code's
technical measures (200-token threshold, two-layer marking, free detection with a <1M-user carve-out,
three EU icons) are carried at **secondary tier** (ppc.land), flagged for first-hand re-verification
against the primary before any load-bearing use.
