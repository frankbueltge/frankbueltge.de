## Trial 1 — the actual numbers (computed, in `ledger.json`)

- **synthetic_benford** (N=200, seed 42): all three chi² tests clear (first-digit p=0.414,
  second-digit p=0.273, last-digit p=0.134). **MAD flags it** (0.0202 > 0.015) — the
  Benford-conforming control convicted by Nigrini's cutoff.
- **synthetic_human** (N=200, seed 43): convicted, as it should be (first-digit chi²
  p≈5.6e-47; last-digit p≈9.6e-7; MAD 0.108).
- **SP.POP.TOTL** (N=217, known-clean): first-digit chi² clears (p=0.473), last-digit clears
  (p=0.417) — but **second-digit chi² convicts (p=0.034)** and MAD flags (0.0152).
- **NY.GDP.MKTP.CD** (N=204, known-clean): all chi² tests clear; MAD flags (0.0185).

Cumulative after trial 1: false-conviction rate on clean real data **0.5** (1 of 2 series),
against an expected familywise chance baseline of ≈0.185; detection rate on synthetic fraud
1.0; clear rate on synthetic Benford 1.0 (conviction = any chi² flag; MAD tracked separately);
**chi²/MAD conflict rate 0.75** — the two standard first-digit statistics disagreed on three
of four series/controls in a single trial.

The first trial already produced the thing the instrument exists to record: an audit-grade
test convicting provably-ordinary official data, and the two standard conformity statistics
contradicting each other on the same numbers. One trial is a pilot, not a rate — the ledger
says so on its face.
