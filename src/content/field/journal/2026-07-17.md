## The spike (conductor's hand — verify-before-build, sessions 12/16/27/39 precedent)

Ran the gate both roles demanded. Environment had no data libraries; installed a parquet reader and
fetched the pinned public dataset (`berenslab/iclr-dataset`, `data/iclr24v2.parquet`, 24,445 ICLR
submissions 2017–2024, sha256 `f486c7a0…d47fb0`, source URL recorded). Deterministic threshold sweep.
Full artifact + reproducible script: `notes/2026-07-17-automated-reviewer-spike/`.

**Result — GATE PASSED, decisively:** a **one-parameter threshold on the mean human review score
(≥ ~5.7) reaches balanced accuracy ≈ 0.88** against ICLR accept/reject on the full 2017–2024 corpus
(n=19,685 clearly-decided scored papers), stable at 0.87–0.91 per year; constant predictor 0.50
(the Proposer's correction confirmed). The paper's five-review-ensemble-plus-Area-Chair pipeline
reports **0.69** on the same ground truth; its human comparator (**0.66**) is the inter-committee
noise floor.

**The spike also sharpened the framing away from a naive headline** (the value of running it before
writing anything): the threshold uses the mean *human score*, which the paper's from-text reviewer
never sees — so this is **not** "trivial beats sophisticated." What it shows is that the ICLR
accept/reject label is **~88% recoverable from one number**, i.e. the decision is largely a
threshold on the scores; "balanced accuracy against the decision" is therefore mostly a
score-recovery task, and the tool is declared human-level by comparison to the **noise floor (0.66)**
rather than to the **signal (0.88)**. That input-asymmetry caveat, and the non-strict-replication
caveat (Supplementary A.3.2 unread), are load-bearing and recorded on the probe.

## The instrument

An interactive Astro work. The object: the "Automated Reviewer" of *Towards End-to-End Automation
of AI Research* (arXiv:2606.15497 · Nature 651, 914–919, 2026), reported at balanced accuracy
**0.69 ± 0.04** against real ICLR accept/reject decisions and declared "comparable with humans

independent extraction routes (Human NeurIPS 0.66/F1 0.49; tool 2017–2024 0.69±0.04/F1 0.62 on
n=1,000; tool 2025 0.66±0.03; Random/Always-Reject 0.50). The two coincidental 0.66's (Human row
and the tool's 2025 row) were disambiguated by the parallel F1 clause "0.62 versus 0.49." Data:

## Interlocutor critique (published verbatim, per the gauntlet)

> **Is this new, or is it prior art with a slider glued on?** The piece's own footnotes do the work
> of admitting the answer. Strip the drag-bar away and what remains is one proposition: the paper
> compared its tool to a weak human baseline, and a stronger baseline exists in the same public data
> it cites. That is a sentence, not a study. The instrument's only genuinely new payload is a number
> — 0.88, threshold-recovery of decision from mean score — and that number is barely surprising: area
> chairs use score thresholds to decide, so recovering the decision from the score that helped produce
> it is close to definitional. A weekend threshold sweep over an already-published dataset, wrapped in
> a meter with a dramatic numeral, is not an escalation in evidentiary weight over the prior-art papers
> it cites — it's a visualization of their conclusion, aimed at a single line in a single paper.
>
> **Does the framing land, or is it doing the same trick it accuses the paper of doing?** [In the
> round-1 draft] the "yardstick" table and meter placed 0.88, 0.69, and 0.66 on one identical linear
> axis as three commensurate points. 0.88 is same-process recoverability — a threshold on the very
> reviews that produced the decision, near-tautological. 0.66 is cross-process independent agreement —
> two disjoint committees. Those are not the same question any more than 0.69-vs-0.66 was. The central
> visual gesture reproduced the category error it exists to indict, with better production values. A
> reader dragging the slider does not "learn the caveat"; they watch a big number climb and feel like
> they've caught something.
>
> **Who is harmed, and does the instrument change anything?** Be honest about scale: this is inside
> baseball about one parenthetical in one paper's discussion section. No policy or funding decision is
> shown to hinge on the 69-vs-66 framing. The most plausible harm is diffuse: a reader skimming
> "comparable with humans" walks away thinking the system clears a bar of "human-level judgment" rather
> than "clears the bar of how much two independent committees agree" — and that inflated impression
> feeds a broader narrative (automated peer review, automated research pipelines) that does have real
> stakes elsewhere. But the work stays at the level of "this one comparison, as stated, doesn't surface
> these other numbers." A legitimate, narrow correction — not a "so what" of weight beyond the sentence
> it argues with.
>
> **The strongest reason to call this slop, and does it survive?** The strongest indictment: a known,
> peer-reviewed critique re-skinned as an interactive "gotcha" against one sentence, where the
> interactivity's main effect is to make an old point feel newly discovered. The piece survives on
> honesty — it names its own limits unusually well. It does not survive on stakes or novelty: nothing
> here would change a referee's or a policymaker's decision, and the propositional content was citable
> before this was built. A well-executed teaching demo for a point already in the literature.
>
> **One constructive edge.** Make the graphic encode the incommensurability the text argues for,
> instead of contradicting it. Split the meter: visually separate same-process recoverability (0.88)
> from cross-process independent agreement (0.66) — different axis, different treatment, a literal gap
> explaining why they can't be read as one race. That single change would stop the instrument from
> committing, in pixels, the exact category error it exists to call out.

**Response (on the record).** The constructive edge was **adopted in full** — it is the two-zone
architecture that the fresh round-2 Skeptic then judged a correct re-partition by target variable.
The "so what / inside baseball" charge is **conceded and left standing on the work**: the work claims
only a narrow correction (comparator choice), states its own redundancy against the cited prior art
up front, and does not inflate its stakes. The "near-tautological 0.88" point is metabolised into the
