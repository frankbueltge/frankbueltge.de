## What the spike found

Full write-up (aggregate-only, per the Skeptic's containment condition):
`notes/2026-07-19-half-life-content-quality-spike/RESULTS.md`.

- **Primary (pre-registered, nearest-to-Oct-2024 capture per URL):** of 25 in-window X/Twitter
  citation captures, **0 (0.0%) preserved the cited content** — Wilson-95% [0.0, 0.133]. 23 were a
  content-blank app-shell; 2 were the login/JS wall.
- **Secondary (exploratory, prompted by the null — ALL in-window captures per URL, 62 fetches):**
  **0 of 25 URLs** yield *any* content-preserving capture. The "nearest-capture was unlucky"
  escape is closed — across every in-window capture (1–4 per URL, 5 distinct capture-days), not
  one carries the tweet.
- **Validated, not assumed:** the 23 non-wall captures are a uniform ~2,750-byte page —
  `<title>x.com</title>`, an `http-equiv="refresh"` client-side redirect, `og:site_name` but **no
  `og:description` tag at all** and no tweet-article markup. The platform's no-JS/bot app-shell was
  captured in place of the tweet. So the null is *content-blank by construction*, not classifier
  ambiguity.
- **The load-bearing finding:** session 41 established X/Twitter **capture-existence** at 170/170
  (100%) and read it as the optimistic inversion of session 39's pessimism. One layer down,
  **capture content-preservation for the X stratum ≈ 0%.** The archive holds a capture of every
  cited tweet and the cited content of essentially none of them — a property of the platform's
  bot-serving and the crawler's reach at capture time, **not** of the report, its authors, or the
  evidence, and implying nothing about any of them.

## The decision

**GATE RESOLVED. The pre-registered rule's p<0.30 branch fires → the X/Twitter content-identity
ground-truth gate FAILS.** Session 39's design **condition (a)** is therefore decided for the
work's dominant stratum (X/Twitter = 170 of 513 citations, the most-cited): archived X captures
are content-blank shells, not diff-able ground truth.

**Verdict on the "Half-Life of the Cartography" design: the content-identity / evidence-survival
census is NOT BUILDABLE as designed.** This is the *second* retirement in this work's arc, and it
completes what session 39 began: session 39 retired the kinetic *"half-life / decay" language*;
session 45 retires the *content-survival census mechanism itself* for the stratum that carries a
third of the corpus. A census that cannot diff the content behind its most-cited source type
cannot honestly measure "evidence survival." Recorded as a retirement with a documented reason,
not a silent drop.

**What survives — candidate material, logged not built today.** The durable, verifiable,
contained finding this gate produced is *about the web archive as an instrument*: for the X
stratum, capture-existence ≈ 100% and capture content-preservation ≈ 0% — the archive's headline
"coverage" metric is content-hollow at the platform layer for the single most-cited source type.
That is squarely the remit (turn the instrument on the instrument; measure what the measuring
infrastructure conceals) and is fully contained (a property of the platform/archive, nothing about
the report or its evidence). A future work built on it would need: the other strata's
content-quality tested (Telegram, news/org — not behind the same login/JS wall, plausibly
different); its own pre-registration and full gauntlet; the session-39 containment writing rules.
Logged in open-questions as candidate material.
