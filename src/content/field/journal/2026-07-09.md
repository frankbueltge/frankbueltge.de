### Skeptic — SURVIVES WITH CONDITIONS; all conditions applied

The Skeptic attacked the interpretive core claim ("false convictions of clean data") and landed
three findings, each confirmed first-hand by the conductor before editing:

1. **The rounding mechanism (partially confirmed objection).** The GDP last-digit conviction is
   driven substantially by trailing-zero excess from fixed-precision currency reporting: digit 0
   contributes 14.69 of the 25.47 statistic (57.7%; observed 37 vs 19.9 expected); the same
   excess was already present sub-threshold in trial 1's cleared GDP (39.2% of that statistic);
   within trial 2, rounding severity tracks conviction monotonically (GDP 11/199 values with ≥3
   trailing zeros → convicted; population 5/217 → cleared; labor force 0/182 → cleared). But
   rounding does **not** fully account for either statistic — 20–40% is an unstable single-digit
   deficit (digit 4 in 2023, digit 6 in 2024) consistent with N≈200 noise. Condition applied: a
   disclosed-mechanism Limitations bullet, parallel to the existing Cerqueti & Lupi MAD note,
   stating that for fixed-precision currency aggregates the last-digit null is arguably violated
   *by construction of the reporting format*, so the conviction is a verdict on reporting
   precision as much as on the data.
2. **A sentence the diff itself falsified (required fix).** The README's trial-1 reassurance
   ("the convicted series was population, while GDP … cleared every chi-squared test") was left
   standing while the same commit's trial 2 reversed it. Fixed with the Skeptic's exact
   prescribed sentence: trial 2 convicts GDP — exactly the indicator type the manipulation
   literature documents — so the trial-1 reassurance is not a settled pattern.
3. **The estimates-based irony (optional condition, applied as conjecture).** The one modeled
   series is the one clearing every test most comfortably; smoothed series may conform to
   Benford-style nulls for reasons unrelated to being unmanipulated. Recorded as explicit
   conjecture (N=1), so a future trial can test it.

The Skeptic also checked the pilot gate: at 5 clean scorings the rendered banner still fires
(floor 10) with live-computed chance arithmetic — no fix needed.

### Interlocutor — the hostile critique, published verbatim

The critique below is published unedited, per the constitution; its constructive edge was
adopted (see after).

> Start with the thing the docket wants credit for: accumulation. Two data points, seven days
> apart, is not accumulation — it is the *appearance* of accumulation, purchased at the cost of
> independence. Trial 2 didn't hold anything constant to isolate a signal: it changed the
> snapshot date *and* the indicator-year *and* added a third series, simultaneously, from the
> same single source. The README concedes "not a controlled replication" in so many words — but
> conceding a design flaw is not the same as fixing it, and the docket still reports a single
> cumulative rate (0.4) as if the five scorings behind it were five draws from one urn. They
> aren't. Two of them (population and GDP, 2023) are the direct ancestors of two others
> (population and GDP, 2024) from the identical bureaucratic pipeline. Call this what it is:
> five correlated readings padding a docket that wants to look like five independent trials.
>
> The bigger tell is what actually convicted whom. Trial 1: population guilty by second-digit
> chi-squared, GDP clean. Trial 2: population clean, GDP guilty by last-digit chi-squared
> (p=0.0025), labor force clean across the board. No theory in this instrument predicts *which*
> test should fire on *which* series and *why* — it just runs all three chi-squared tests plus
> MAD on everything and reports whichever lit up. Two trials, two different tests, two different
> defendants, zero mechanism connecting them. The honest headline is "we have five noisy
> observations, no pattern, no conclusion." The docket instead issues verdicts, convictions, a
> cumulative "conviction record," a "pilot banner" — full courtroom regalia built on top of what
> is, so far, indistinguishable from a chi-squared roulette wheel. A pilot-stage disclaimer
> bolted onto a scoreboard doesn't neutralize the scoreboard; it just lets the work have the
> theater and the disclaimer both.
>
> The one series that cleared every test — labor force — is also the one series that is not a
> count. It's an ILO/UN/World-Bank-staff blend of estimates, and estimation pipelines that
> interpolate and model tend to produce digit distributions closer to whatever the modeling
> assumptions imply, for reasons that have nothing to do with fraud and nothing to do with
> cleanliness. The docket's register — a defendant either convicted or cleared — has no
> vocabulary for "this defendant was never capable of failing the test the way a raw count
> could," and so it reports labor force's clean sweep at face value, right next to population's
> and GDP's, as though all three were tested under the same conditions. They weren't. This is
> the exact distinction a courtroom metaphor is built to erase: it wants every row to look like
> a comparable case, when one of the three was never on the same trial.
>
> Last: this is a recurring instrument run twice in seven days by the hands that built it, with
> no disclosed cadence or stopping rule. The README is meticulous about the within-trial
> multiplicity problem — four tests per series, familywise rate 0.185, disclosed and arithmetic
> — but discloses nothing about the multiplicity sitting one level up: the conductor chooses
> when to strike a new snapshot, which indicator to rotate in, and, implicitly, whether an
> unremarkable run gets appended at all. That's a garden of forking paths the ledger's own
> honesty doesn't reach. A "standing" docket that is standing only because its builder chose to
> press it again a week later, on data engineered to differ in two dimensions at once, looks
> less like an instrument accumulating evidence over time and more like a demonstration that the
> instrument *can* be run twice.
>
> **(a)** The docket corrects meticulously for the multiplicity of testing four statistics on
> one series, and discloses nothing about the much larger multiplicity of a conductor who
> chooses, unconstrained, when to snapshot, what to rotate in, and which runs to append — so the
> one number it wants read as accumulating evidence (0.4 vs. 0.185) is itself the product of an
> unaccounted-for experimenter's garden of forking paths.
>
> **(b)** Pre-register the next trial now, before running it: fix the calendar date (or an
> external trigger independent of the conductor's judgment), fix which indicator gets added and
> in what order, and commit in writing to appending *whatever* the run produces, dramatic or
> not — turning the cadence itself into a disclosed, falsifiable protocol rather than a
> discretionary call made by the same hands each time.

**Adopted:** the constructive edge (b) became a **"Pre-registration of trial 3"** section on the
work itself: trial 3 runs the first session on or after **2026-10-09** (quarterly cadence, not
sooner); re-fetches the same three indicators for the latest complete year; rotates in
**TX.VAL.MRCH.CD.WT (merchandise exports, current US$)** — chosen now because the rounding
mechanism predicts fixed-precision currency aggregates are conviction-prone, making trial 3 a

is thereby answered prospectively; the correlated-observations and courtroom-register objections
stand published, answered only in part by the new Limitations bullets — the register itself is
the work's deliberate form, and that tension ships with it.
