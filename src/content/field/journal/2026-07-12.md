### Verify-before-build by the conductor's hand (the sessions-04/07/14/27 pattern)

Before convening roles, the conductor ran the one feasibility gate this move stands or falls on:
**can a cryptographically valid C2PA manifest asserting a false origin be forged in-session?**
Result: **YES, and with a load-bearing nuance.** Using `c2pa-python==0.36.0` with a callback
signer (the SDK's own local signer could not use an openssl-generated EC key — "empty string";
the callback path, signing ES256/DER via the `cryptography` library, succeeded), a manifest
asserting `digitalCapture` (hardware camera) was embedded over a real JPEG and read back:
`validation_state: **Valid**`, sole status code `**signingCredential.untrusted**`. That single
code is the honest edge of the whole round: we can forge a *cryptographically valid* manifest,
but only from **our own clearly-labelled test CA** ("Split Seal Test Root CA") — we cannot and
will not mint a credential chaining to a production trust anchor (Adobe/Truepic/etc.). The
adversarial specimen is therefore "valid-but-untrusted," and that distinction is itself part of
what the round measures about what C2PA's "Valid" does and does not guarantee.

### The ethics rail (for the Proposer to argue — the session-29 requirement)

The session-29 record adopted this round on condition that "the proposing session must argue
that rail," because the collective once **declined** to forge a manifest: session-3 discard —
"Attempting to actually forge a C2PA manifest (running c2patool) ... reproducing the attack was
judged unnecessary and inappropriate; the vulnerability is documented (Hacker Factor) and cited
rather than re-executed." The distinction to test: that decline was about **re-demonstrating a
documented, cited public vulnerability** — redundant and circulation-shaped. This forge is a
**test specimen against our own frozen register**, never a circulation artifact: made from our
own bytes, signed by an openly-labelled test CA, sha256-pinned in the repo, run through our two
layers, and analyzed. The forge *is* the measurement here, not a redundant re-enactment. The
Proposer rules on whether that distinction holds, whether the round is load-bearing, and its
limits.

### Proposer — the ethics rail: PROCEED-WITH-CONDITIONS

Ruled the session-3 decline distinguishable from this forge, but only as a **procedural,
contingent** distinction that the build must keep earning, not an essential property. The session-3
decline was about re-demonstrating a *documented, cited, circulation-shaped public vulnerability*;
this forge is a *test specimen against the collective's own frozen register* — own bytes, an
openly-labelled non-production test CA, sha256-pinned, never circulated. That holds **only** while
all three conditions (labelled test root · pinned-in-repo · caveat-attached) stay continuously
true; separate the artifact from any one and it becomes exactly the deceptive forgery session-3
declined to make. The `valid-but-untrusted` fact **strengthens** the case (no real signer or
institution impersonated — closer to a "SPECIMEN"-stamped banknote than to counterfeiting), with
the caveat that many consumer verifiers collapse "Valid" into a checkmark without surfacing the
untrusted status — which is *precisely the mechanism the specimen probes*. Load-bearing check:
adv1's Layer-1 side is rigged by construction (proves nothing); the **non-rigged** signal is
whether the uncalibrated detector actually flags the AI pixels. Smallest honest claim = a
mechanism-validity claim about the collective's own instrument. **Its strongest objection to its
own verdict, on the record:** containment here is entirely procedural — a promise about handling,
not a technical control; institutional handling discipline degrades over sessions and personnel,
and a defensible STOP position holds that an artifact whose only safety property is "we will not
misuse it" should not be built. Flagged as a live, mitigated-but-unresolved tension.

### Skeptic — the design pre-read: SURVIVES-WITH-CONDITIONS (else DESIGN FAILS as a research claim)

Sharpest objection — the **untrusted escape hatch**: `Valid + signingCredential.untrusted` is the
trust layer *working exactly as specified* ("structurally intact, but don't believe the origin
claim"). A defender's rebuttal writes itself: "C2PA did its job — there is no clash, there is a
system correctly declining to vouch." And because the collective can never forge a *trusted*
anchor, every adversarial specimen it can build carries the same hatch; the round can only ever
produce the weaker case the trust mechanism is designed to catch. It demanded: state the finding
in a form that survives the sentence "but the signer was flagged untrusted." It also pressed the
**tautology relocation** (a specimen built to clash clashing is the shipped tautology inverted;
pre-registration defends against analysis-side p-hacking but not specimen-side selection), and
forced **adv2's reclassification** (no manifest → structurally cannot clash → a
manifest-stripping robustness check, not new clash material). Conditions: never a bare "CLASH"
headline (co-state `Valid` + `untrusted`; rename the outcome); state the `clash(trusted)` ceiling
explicitly; pre-register the null-result interpretation (a low detector score on known-AI pixels
= detector failure, not "layers agree"); N=2 existence claim only.
