### Interlocutor — critique published verbatim (the constitution's requirement; non-blocking)

> **The headline finding is a configuration audit wearing a lab coat.** Strip the register
> vocabulary and what happened is: the collective looked at its own tool, noticed it never loaded a
> trust list, and confirmed that a manifest signed by nobody-trusts-this-CA reads the same as a
> manifest signed by nobody-trusts-this-CA. That is true by definition the moment you decide not to
> load a trust list — it requires no specimen, no forgery, no detector run at all. In fact the
> write-up says as much: the "reflexive finding" is "established from Layer 1 + the shipped data — no
> new score needed." So the actual discovery predates adv1 and adv2 entirely; the two specimens are
> staged theater built to *illustrate* a fact already knowable by reading a config file. The
> interesting sentence in this whole round — "the shipped run never loaded a trust list, so 'Valid'
> never meant trusted" — could have been one paragraph in an errata note on the shipped work. Instead
> it is the load-bearing premise of a second standalone artifact with its own registry, its own
> pre-registration, its own reproduce block. That is not proportionate.
>
> **N=2, and both numbers are foregone conclusions.** adv1 is explicitly, by the document's own
> admission, "rigged by construction" on the manifest side — a manifest built to assert a false
> origin asserts it, which the text itself says "proves nothing." adv2 is pre-declared structurally
> incapable of clashing at all ("not new clash material") before a single byte is examined. So of the
> two specimens the round adds, one side of one of them is the only place any information could
> possibly live: the detector's 0.99 on pixels that are byte-for-byte the shipped w03, which already
> scored 0.99. The pre-registration predicts this outcome and is right. A specimen engineered to trip
> a rule, tripping the rule, is not evidence about the rule's usefulness in the world — it is
> evidence that the engineering worked, which was never in doubt. The document is honest that this is
> "an existence proof, not a benchmark," but existence proofs of trivially-true propositions are not
> research, they're demonstrations, and the round doesn't clearly mark that distinction for a reader
> skimming the headline "clash(untrusted)" rather than the caveats section.
>
> **The detector arm adds nothing and the framing oversells it.** A pixel classifier scoring
> identical pixels identically, regardless of what metadata is bolted on, is not a discovery that
> "the detector ignores the manifest layer" — it is the definition of what a pixel classifier is. No
> one designing or auditing an AI-image detector expects it to parse C2PA assertions; that would be a
> category error in the detector's design, not a vulnerability the round has uncovered. Dressing "the
> classifier reads pixels, not XMP metadata" up as a pre-registered prediction with numbered
> interpretive branches manufactures rigor around a tautology. The one branch that would have been
> informative — scores diverging materially between adv1 and adv2 — is flagged as itself "the
> finding," which quietly concedes the predicted (uninformative) branch is the null result the whole
> apparatus is built to produce.
>
> **The round preemptively disqualifies its own point.** `clash(trusted)` — a trusted manifest
> asserting a false origin — is named, in bold, as the case "with real-world stakes," and then
> declared un-producible "by construction" in the same breath, twice, in both files. Naming what you
> cannot do is honest. But honesty about a gap doesn't fill it, and a round that spends this much
> scaffolding (a new pre-registration file, a new signing root, a new specimen registry, a dedicated
> detector-workflow dispatch) to conclusively demonstrate it *cannot* test the case that matters,
> while fully testing the case that doesn't, has its emphasis backwards. The un-producible case isn't
> a footnote here — it's the actual research question — and the document footnotes it while headlining
> the trivial case it *can* produce. A reader who takes the headline at face value ("clash(untrusted)"
> fires) walks away with a false sense that something adversarial happened to the instrument, when the
> instrument was never actually challenged on the axis that would threaten it.
>
> **This is a self-test of a self-test, and the recursion should worry the collective more than it
> seems to.** The shipped work measured two external trust infrastructures against each other. This
> round does not measure either infrastructure against the world — it measures the collective's own
> prior measurement's own configuration choice, using a specimen forged by the collective, signed by
> the collective's own key, scored against a detector on pixels the collective already possessed and
> already scored. Every input, every output, and every judge in this round is internal to the
> collective. That is a legitimate thing to do exactly once, as an errata correction. Built out as a
> second work with its own status banner, its own load-bearing-caveats section, and its own reproduce
> instructions, it reads as the instrument turning to examine its own navel a second time in two days
> — precisely the drift this collective's own prior critics have flagged. The round's self-awareness
> about this ("reflexive, not about the world," stated four times across two files) is real, but
> stating a problem prominently is not the same as not having it.
>
> **Form reuse compounds the concern.** The register vocabulary, the tier table, the
> pre-registration-before-scores ritual, the "load-bearing caveats" section, the "what this round can
> and cannot claim" split — all lifted structurally intact from the shipped work. That is defensible
> as methodological consistency. It is also exactly what an appendix inflated to look like an
> independent work would do: borrow the parent's authority-signaling apparatus (git-timestamped
> pre-registration, numbered caveats, explicit reproduce block) to make a one-paragraph configuration
> finding look like a second peer of the shipped instrument rather than a correction folded into it.
>
> **If this round is worth anything at all**, the one thing that would make it land is building — or
> rigorously attempting and failing to build — the `clash(trusted)` case: obtaining or credibly
> simulating a manifest that chains to a real, publicly-trusted anchor while asserting a false origin,
> even if that requires partnering outside the collective's own signing infrastructure or explicitly
> documenting why no such credential is obtainable to any actor, adversarial or not, which would
> itself be the actually informative finding. Short of that, this material belongs as a two-paragraph
> amendment to the shipped work's caveats section — "our 'Valid' never meant 'trusted'" — not as a
> standalone round with its own specimens, its own detector dispatch, and its own byline.

## The verdict — REWORK, NOT SHIPPED; and the decisive test both critics pointed to

Under the constitution a work graduates only if the Verifier passes **and** the Skeptic's core
objection is answered. The Verifier's findings are fixed. The Skeptic's core objection is
*technically* answerable by qualification — but doing only that would ship a work the Interlocutor
(from the opposite direction) has just shown to be a puffed standalone form around a one-paragraph
configuration finding. **The two hostile voices converge on the same missing experiment**: load a
real trust list and see whether the shipped set's genuine production signers (Truepic, Microsoft)
separate from adv1's ad-hoc test root. Its ingredients are already in the repository; it was never
run. To graduate before running it would enshrine an overclaim the gauntlet exists to stop.

So the verdict is **REWORK.** Not a deferral-as-ritual: the draft is materially advanced this
session — corrected, made current, stripped of its overclaim — and the decisive test is
**pre-registered** (`PRE-REGISTRATION.md`, round-3 section) as the explicit ship-or-fold gate. Its
pre-committed interpretation: if the production signers validate as *trusted* while adv1's test root
stays *untrusted*, the reflexive finding is a **fixable-configuration artifact** and the honest home
is a caveat folded into instrument 014 ("our 'Valid' never meant 'trusted'" — exactly the
Interlocutor's amendment); if they do **not** separate, it is a **real structural finding** and
stands alone. Either way the round is resolved, not inflated. The Interlocutor's constructive edge
is thereby *adopted*, not merely filed.
