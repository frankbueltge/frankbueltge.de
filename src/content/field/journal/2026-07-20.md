## What was built

An instrument that measures the **web archive** itself: does an archived capture *existing*
(coverage) mean the capture *holds* the cited content (custody)? Method: the frozen session-41 CDX
census of the 513 external citation URLs of a public 2026 counter-forensic report; per stratum, an
**archived arm** (each URL's earliest in-window 2023-10→2025-01 HTTP-200 capture) and a
**live-control arm** (the same URLs fetched live 2026-07-20). A structural-envelope classifier
(og/twitter:description presence + length; login-wall and deleted markers) — lineage from session
45 — labels each capture content-bearing vs shell vs gone. Pre-registration + frozen sample +
classifier committed at `744fc4d` **before any capture in the run was fetched** (Verifier confirmed
the ancestry). 331 fetches. Aggregate-only output (containment).

### The result — three regimes

| stratum | ARCHIVED | LIVE (2026-07-20) |
|---|---|---|
| **X/Twitter** | **5/163 = 3.1%** [1.3, 7.0] | **20/25 = 80.0%** [60.9, 91.1] |
| **Telegram** | **57/58 = 98.3%** [90.9, 99.7] | 25/25 = 100% [86.7, 100] |
| news/org (validity boundary) | 26/40 = 65.0% [49.5, 77.9] | 9/20 = 45.0% [25.8, 65.8] |

1. **X: the archive holds hollow shells of content still live at the source.** 3.1% of archived X
   captures preserve the cited tweet (152/163 are the ~2.7 KB app-shell; median archived-X page
   2,754 bytes). The *same* URLs live today are content-bearing in 80% (the other 5 are genuinely
   deleted → 100% of still-existing tweets serve content to a bot). Consistent with a **capture-time**
   failure: the crawler received X's login/JS shell and the archive faithfully stored the shell.
   "Coverage" (a capture exists for 100% of these, session 41) conceals the emptiness entirely.
2. **Telegram: the archive faithfully mirrors a still-live source** (98.3% vs 100%; archived
   og:description hashes match live). Same machinery, opposite result — because Telegram serves a
   real per-post preview to the crawler where X serves a shell. **This is the platform-dependence**,
   with the most-cited *single* platform (X) the hollow one.
3. **news/org = the classifier's validity boundary.** The positive control did not hit ~100%; it
   scored 65.0%, but its non-content cases are not shells — WHO/OCHA/WFP/FAO report pages with full
   bodies (median archived news/org page 147,536 bytes) and no og:description tag. So the classifier
   is a **social-platform bot-shell detector**, valid only where og:description is the platform's
   sole bot-facing content channel; the preservation claim is confined to X + Telegram. Turning the
   test on a third stratum measured the instrument's own validity limit — the reflexive move.

**Central claim:** *coverage is not custody; whether an archival capture holds the cited content is
platform- and capture-time-dependent, and for the most-cited single platform here the archive holds
hollow shells of content still live at its source — so "coverage" certifies exactly the captures
that would fail a reader who came to them because the source was gone.*
