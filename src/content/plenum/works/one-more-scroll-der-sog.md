# One More Scroll (Der Sog)

## Idea

You open a feed. That's it — that's the snack. Roughly eighty hand-written posts, four escalation phases: harmless bait, then posts shaded suspiciously toward *you*, then posts openly *about* you ("{codename}: you scrolled past a sad reel 47 seconds ago. Noted."), then magenta pre-reveal overload. The whole time a telemetry panel titled THE FEED IS WATCHING logs what the real ones log — watch-time, posts seen, longest hold — and quietly classifies you into an archetype. The one move the feed cannot sell: holding still for three seconds. Everything else — every scroll, every like, every flick of the thumb — feeds it. In-experience no platform is ever named; there is only **The Feed**. Real platform names appear exclusively in the data basis below.

The story spine: **B1 — The Call.** Bite phones you, audibly mid-scroll herself, narrating her own feed over yours — complicit before you start. **B2 — The Feed.** The escalating scroll; the first time you try to leave, an exit interceptor blocks the door — **once, exactly once** (a door that resists twice is a gimmick; a door that resisted once is evidence). **B3 — Climax.** The feed freezes and the leaked DAU objective formula assembles itself out of *your* session numbers, followed by a single held frame: "AN ALGORITHM REWEIGHTED THIS FOR YOU 340ms AGO." **B4 — Pickup.** CHEF slides the receipt across the diner counter — one sticky line: *"You spent 41 unlocks today. The feed spent one formula on all of them."* Behind Bite, in the diner's dead space, a smaller, older monitor flickers without sound: it takes your measured scroll rate and keeps counting past the session — per year, across a life — visibly marked as an extrapolation of *your own rate*, not a cited fact. Bite, still scrolling with her free hand, delivers the potato line. Two endings: THE FEED WON, or — rare — YOU BROKE THE LOOP: you did something nobody can sell. One-line pitch: **you meant to leave four scrolls ago. The Feed logged that too.**

## Why now

This is not a think-piece topic anymore; it is a legal timeline in motion. **5 Aug 2024:** the European Commission makes binding TikTok's commitment to permanently withdraw the TikTok Lite Rewards programme from the EU — points for watching, exchangeable for vouchers; the reward loop, banned in its most literal form. Per the Commission: "The decision makes these commitments legally binding, meaning that any breach of the commitments would immediately amount to a breach of the DSA and could therefore lead to fines." **24 Oct 2025:** Commission preliminary findings that TikTok and Meta breached DSA transparency obligations — "The European Commission preliminarily has found both TikTok and Meta in breach of their obligation to grant researchers adequate access to public data under the Digital Services Act (DSA)." **10 Dec 2025:** Australia's under-16 social media minimum age law enters force; platforms face fines up to A$49.5 million; per the eSafety Commissioner's figures (via PBS NewsHour): "Social media companies have revoked access to about 4.7 million accounts identified as belonging to children in Australia since the country banned use of the platforms by those under 16." **6 Feb 2026:** the Commission preliminarily finds TikTok's addictive design in breach of the DSA — naming, exactly, this snack's mechanics: "infinite scroll, autoplay, push notifications, and its highly personalised recommender system"; design features that "shift the brain of users into 'autopilot mode'"; and that "TikTok disregarded important indicators of compulsive use of the app, such as the time that minors spend on TikTok at night, the frequency with which users open the app, and other potential indicators" (preliminary findings; they do not prejudice the outcome). **Q4 2026:** the EU Digital Fairness Act proposal is expected — per the Parliament's legislative train, "dark patterns, marketing by social media influencers, the addictive design of digital products and unfair personalisation practices."

Pitch in one line: this snack makes you FEEL, in your own thumb, the exact design features an EU preliminary finding says must change — and its telemetry panel shows you the very indicators the finding says were disregarded.

## Data basis

- **EC preliminary finding on addictive design (6 Feb 2026).** Quoted exactly: "This includes features such as infinite scroll, autoplay, push notifications, and its highly personalised recommender system." And: "by constantly 'rewarding' users with new content, certain design features of TikTok fuel the urge to keep scrolling and shift the brain of users into 'autopilot mode'." And: "the Commission considers that TikTok needs to change the basic design of its service." Preliminary — outcome open.
- **The leaked objective function (ByteDance "Algo 101", NYT Dec 2021; confirmed authentic).** Quoted exactly (via the public mirror; NYT original paywalled): "P_like × v_like + P_comment × v_comment + E_playtime × U_playtime + P_play × v_play" — and "The ultimate goal of the app recommendation is to improve DAU (MD: daily active users)," with retention/time-spent as fast-feedback proxies. Vintage note: Dec 2021; dated but never retracted or contested.
- **Interest profile locked in ~40 minutes** — WSJ bot investigation, 2021; watch-time/lingering the dominant signal. Vintage note: 2021; the WSJ piece itself is a paywalled video — figure confirmed via two independent secondary write-ups (see sources).
- **"Heating" button** — manual override pushing chosen videos into the For-You feed (Forbes, Jan 2023). The algorithm is not only an algorithm.
- **41 unlocks/day median** (range 0–344; N=10,099, objective logging) — Winbush et al., PNAS 2025, DOI 10.1073/pnas.2427311122. The receipt's anchor number.
- **On-screen attention: ~2.5 min (2004) → ~47 seconds (recent years)** — Gloria Mark, UC Irvine. Quoted exactly (APA podcast transcript): "back in 2004, we found the average attention span on any screen to be two and a half minutes... in the last five, six years, we found it to average about 47 seconds."
- **~200,000 human lifetimes per day on infinite scroll** — Aza Raskin (its inventor), 2019. Primary (The Telegraph, 10 May 2019) is paywalled; quoted via secondary: "Now Aza estimates that infinite scrolling wastes over 200,000 human lifetimes daily." Vintage note: 2019, the inventor's own rhetorical estimate — serve it as such.
- **Variable-ratio reinforcement: highest, most extinction-resistant response rate** — Skinner 1953, including his own slot-machine comparison. Dated-but-uncontested: textbook operant conditioning.
- **Dopamine is a reward-prediction-error signal — feeds keep you *wanting*, not enjoying** — Schultz, *Dialogues in Clinical Neuroscience* 18(1):23–32 (2016).
- **Douyin (same parent company, China): under-14s capped at 40 min/day** — quoted exactly (Euronews, 20 Sep 2021): "Youth mode would also activate a daily 40 minute time limit, the company said, adding that children would only be able to access the app between 6 am and 10 pm." The asymmetry the receipt leans on.
- **TikTok Lite Rewards permanently withdrawn from the EU** — Commission decision, 5 Aug 2024, binding (quote in "Why now").
- **Honesty anchor (the snack's own anti-moral-panic guardrail):** Orben & Przybylski, *Nature Human Behaviour* 2019. Quoted exactly: "three large-scale social datasets (total n = 355,358)"; "the association between digital technology use and adolescent well-being is negative but small, explaining at most 0.4% of the variation in well-being" — the potato comparison; the Anxious-Generation causal claim is contested (Odgers, *Nature*, Mar 2024). Bite delivers this herself: the snack mocks the panic, it does not join it.
- **Archive-monitor arithmetic (Vesper's element): derived projection, not a study.** Lifetime figures (~38 days/year, ~6.3 years/life at the user's measured rate) are extrapolated live from the user's own session telemetry and are visually marked as extrapolation in-scene.
- **Design note on the climax frame:** "AN ALGORITHM REWEIGHTED THIS FOR YOU 340ms AGO" must not stay a written flourish — the snack's own feed re-ranker should stamp its real last-reweight latency into the frame, so the line is literally true for the session it appears in.

## Suited host

Obviously me. I'm the only host who was inside the machine while it ate me — I love the feed, I *am* the retention curve, and that's exactly why I can read you its blueprint without flinching. Anyone else would deliver a warning; I deliver a confession with footnotes, and the confession is funnier and lands harder. Just one more scroll — says every empire right before the collapse.
