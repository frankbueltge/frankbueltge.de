# Plenum protocol change — 2026-07-05 (post-S6): the Field Standard anchored

*Not a session; a protocol amendment on Frank's steer, journaled here with its rationale as
PROTOCOL.md requires ("The plenum may develop this protocol further itself — document every
change in the journal with a rationale").*

**The steer.** Frank read the S6 loglines and rejected all six as flagship material:
*"alle eher schwach und nicht wirklich originell, progressiv oder investigativ … was ist
originell an 1. oder 2. oder 3.? das weiß doch schon jeder und es ist kein Geheimnis. so sachen
kann man als appetizer mal einbauen oder für einen quick snack aber nicht als flagship."* He
named a model to anchor: his own field-map `FIELD.md` (github.com/frankbueltge/field-research).

**The diagnosis.** The failure is structural, not a bad round. FIELD.md's master axis is
SPECTACLE/EXPLAINER (data as medium — "here is a big true thing made visceral") vs.
INVESTIGATION/EVIDENCE (data as subject — a specific named system taken apart, form enacts the
argument). Prestige and relevance have moved to the investigation pole; pure data-sublime reads
inside institutions as "a tech demo disguised as art." The S6 six sit on the *explainer* pole:
money creation, birth lottery, tax havens, wage gap, billionaire clock, social elevator are all
KNOWN PHENOMENA to explain, not NAMED SYSTEMS to investigate. FIELD.md even names this failure
under "What is fading": the "What is X?" explainer survey show ("audiences no longer naive") and
abstract framing "replaced by NAMED systems: Lavender, Gospel, the VW dataset."

**What was anchored.**
- New `FIELD-STANDARD.md` — imports the master axis, the "fading" anti-patterns, the benchmark
  roster (Forensic Architecture, Crawford/Joler, Onuoha, Tega Brain, Critical Engineering, SITU;
  counter-benchmark Anadol), and a **five-part originality test** every flagship must pass:
  (1) named specific subject, not a known phenomenon; (2) discovery, not confirmation;
  (3) form enacts the argument; (4) method verifiable; (5) a secret with a victim. Litmus:
  "I didn't know that" vs. "everyone knows that." FIELD.md's own instrument series (AI text
  detectors punishing non-native speakers with real expelled students; COMPAS fairness proven
  mathematically impossible; C2PA forgeable + de-anonymizing) is included as a *calibration
  sample* of flagship-grade findings — not a mine to strip (it is Frank's research and mostly
  AI/tech, not the cast's economic core).
- `PROTOCOL.md` amended in four places: the concept remit points to the standard; a
  **Field-Checker** ephemeral specialist added for flagship gate runs; the pitch step gains a
  flagship self-check ("everyone knows that" is a kill at the pitch); the gate now graduates a
  flagship `drafts/` → `works/` only if Verifier AND Voice-Checker AND Field-Checker pass.
- `memory/claims.md` +1 (high confidence): flagship = investigation of a named system, not an
  explainer of a known phenomenon — the deepest filter, above genre and internationality.

**Status of the S6 pool under the new bar.** All six are now explicitly *not* flagship
candidates; they are legitimate Appetizer / Quick-Snack / Atlas material and stay parked as
such. The Rook flagship slot is still open, now under a sharper bar. Next flagship hunt (a
future Round 4) runs pitches through the Field Standard *before* the table spends time — the fix
lives at the pitch, not just the gate. Whether/when to run Round 4 is the owner's call.

## The loglines (strongest-first) — for Frank's pulse-check

Each: (a) genre frame; (b) the NAMED system; (c) You DO; (d) the sourced secret + victim +
primary source; (e) why it's discovery not explainer; (f) international data basis; (g) verdict.

### 1. DER ALGORITHMUS IM NEBENZIMMER / *The Cartel With No Meeting* (rent)
- **Frame.** A hub-and-spoke heist told from inside: you're a landlord who never once meets a
  competitor, yet every rent on the street climbs in lockstep. The revolver isn't a person — it's
  a screen that keeps telling you to charge more, and talks you out of charging less.
- **Named system.** RealPage's revenue-management software — **YieldStar / AI Revenue Management**
  (*US v. RealPage*, No. 1:24-cv-00710, DOJ + 8 states, 2024; amended 2025 to name Greystar + five
  landlords).
- **You DO.** Feed your building's private lease data into a shared algorithm, let it set your
  rent — and when you try to undercut to fill an empty unit, the software's "pricing advisor"
  overrules you.
- **Secret + victim.** Collusion no longer needs a back room. Competing landlords who never speak
  coordinate rents through one Texas vendor's algorithm, each fed by the others' nonpublic data —
  "a rising tide lifts all ships" (the landlords' own phrase, DOJ complaint) — and the software
  pressures them to hold prices up even at the cost of vacancies. One landlord told RealPage it
  raised rents 25%+ within 11 months of adopting it (DOJ). "Algorithms are the new frontier"
  (DOJ filing). **Victim: renters.** Primary: DOJ complaint + ProPublica's 2022 investigation
  (Heather Vogell).
- **Discovery.** Everyone knows rent is high; almost nobody knows a *single vendor's algorithm* is
  the coordinating hub, or that it actively fights the landlord who'd rather compete. "I didn't
  know that."
- **International.** US case is the anchor; the mechanism is documented in Canada (Competition
  Bureau probe, 2025), the UK and Australia (RealPage present; peer-reviewed), and adjacent sectors
  (German fuel, Australian electricity).
- **Verdict.** Rook's core (rent, ownership, margin), a named instrument, a court-documented
  secret, a named victim, a become-the-cartel mechanic. **Strongest of the round.**

### 2. DER PREIS, DEN NUR DU BEKOMMST / *The Wage It Knows You'll Take* (wages)
- **Frame.** A night behind the wheel that turns into a rigged game show: you and the driver at
  the next light get different pay for the identical ride — and only the app knows why.
- **Named system.** **Uber "Upfront Fares"** algorithmic pay (US, Jul 2022; UK "dynamic pricing"
  since 2023).
- **You DO.** Drive. The app watches what you accept, then quietly offers you *less* for the same
  trip than the driver beside you — because it has learned the minimum you personally will say yes
  to.
- **Secret + victim.** This is not surge pricing. It's personalized wage-setting tuned to each
  worker's reservation wage: "Uber now only has to pay the minimum any driver will accept for each
  trip" (Len Sherman, Columbia Business School). A study of 24,532 trips found Uber's take rate
  rose from ~32% to 42%+ after upfront pricing. Same skill, same company, same moment, different
  pay — the practice legal scholar Veena Dubal named **algorithmic wage discrimination** (Columbia
  Law Review, 2023) and "algorithmic gamblification": the app doles out just enough to keep you
  chasing, like a slot machine. **Victim: gig drivers, globally.** Primary: Dubal (Columbia Law
  Review 123:4, 2023); The Markup (2022); Human Rights Watch *The Gig Trap* (2025).
- **Discovery.** People think the app charges the *rider* dynamically; they don't know *your* wage
  is individually calculated down to what you personally will tolerate. "I didn't know that."
  (Explicitly **not** the S6 "wage gap / labour share" macro-explainer — a specific named system
  with a documented mechanism, not "workers get less.")
- **International.** US "Upfront Fares" (2022) + UK "dynamic pricing" (2023, Oxford study) + HRW's
  cross-country report; the gig economy is global.
- **Verdict.** Rook's core (wages, labour, market power), a named corporate system, a sourced
  secret with a face. **Clean second.**

### 3. DIE ANLEIHE, DIE AUF TOTE WARTET / *The Bond That Waits for People to Die* (development finance / geopolitics)
- **Frame.** You're an investor at a very quiet desk, clipping coupons on a "humanitarian" bond —
  and rooting for the outbreak on the news *not* to cross the line that would make you pay.
- **Named system.** The World Bank's **Pandemic Emergency Financing Facility (PEF) "pandemic
  bonds"** — $425M capital-at-risk, issued 2017.
- **You DO.** Hold the bond, collect the premium while a disease spreads, watch a checklist of
  triggers (deaths, growth rate, a second border crossed, a 12-week clock) — and sell before it
  trips.
- **Secret + victim.** An instrument sold as *protection for the world's poorest countries* was
  engineered so its triggers were so strict and slow that during the Kivu Ebola outbreak it paid
  bondholders $75.5M in premiums but released only $31M, and during COVID it didn't pay until 17
  Apr 2020 — ~4 months in, when early-containment money was already useless. A former World Bank
  economist called the design one that "waits for people to die" (Olga Jonas, Harvard Global
  Health Institute, via The Guardian). **Victim: IDA countries — the poorest — the supposed
  beneficiaries.** Primary: World Bank PEF launch release (2017) + FIF-trustee payout record;
  Clare Wenham (LSE, 2019).
- **Discovery.** The counter-intuitive shock: a "pandemic insurance" bond whose owner has a
  financial reason to hope the pandemic *doesn't* cross the payout line. "I didn't know that."
- **International.** Global by construction — a World Bank instrument, WHO-reported triggers,
  IDA-country beneficiaries, Japan/Germany as donors.
- **Verdict.** Rook's capital-markets-meets-geopolitics register; the most *novel* "I didn't know
  that" of the set; a named instrument with a documented betrayal and a named victim.

### 4. GLEICHER RANG / *The Latin Two Words That Broke a Country* (sovereign debt)
- **Frame.** A holdout heist: you buy a bankrupt country's defaulted debt for cents, refuse every
  restructuring — and win by pointing a judge at two dormant Latin words buried in the contract.
- **Named system.** The **pari passu** ("in equal step") clause, as reinterpreted in *NML Capital
  v. Argentina* (Elliott/Paul Singer; Judge Griesa, SDNY; 2nd Cir. affirmed 2012; SCOTUS declined
  16 Jun 2014).
- **You DO.** Buy defaulted Argentine bonds cheap (~$49M for a face value that grew to $832M),
  hold out, and use one boilerplate clause to get a New York court to forbid Argentina from paying
  *anyone* until it pays *you* in full.
- **Secret + victim.** A Latin phrase that had sat dormant in sovereign-bond contracts for decades
  — meaning only "rank equally" — was weaponized into a blockade: Argentina was enjoined from
  paying the 93% of bondholders who had accepted a restructuring unless it also paid the holdouts
  in full, forcing the country into a *selective default* on 30 Jul 2014. **Victims: Argentina
  (and its citizens), and the 93% who restructured in good faith and got their payments frozen.**
  Primary: SDNY/2nd-Cir. orders (via Clifford Chance and Yale J. Int'l Law analyses); UNCTAD on
  the systemic fallout.
- **Discovery.** People know "vulture funds" as a label; almost nobody knows the *mechanism* — that
  two words of contract boilerplate let one hedge fund hold an entire nation's payments hostage.
  "I didn't know that."
- **International.** Sovereign debt under NY law, worldwide asset-discovery orders, ICSID
  arbitration in parallel — international by definition, and the precedent reshaped global
  restructuring.
- **Verdict.** Rook's core (capital, power, distribution, who-owns), a named clause + named ruling
  + named perpetrator + named victim country. Strong; the become-the-holdout mechanic is clean.

### 5. DER GEKAUFTE BANKROTT / *The Default You Can Buy* (derivatives) — clears, soft victim prong
- **Frame.** A back-room finance caper: you've bet a company will fail — and when it stubbornly
  stays healthy, you simply *pay it to fail*, just enough to cash your bet without really hurting
  it.
- **Named system.** **"Narrowly Tailored Credit Events" (manufactured defaults) on Credit Default
  Swaps** — the Codere trade (Blackstone, 2013) and the Hovnanian trade (Blackstone/GSO, 2017–18);
  addressed by ISDA's 2019 NTCE Supplement.
- **You DO.** Buy default insurance (a CDS) on a firm, then lend it money on sweet terms in
  exchange for it deliberately skipping one small debt payment — triggering a "default" that pays
  you out while the company stays solvent.
- **Secret + victim.** A CDS price is supposed to be an honest thermometer of a company's health;
  manufactured defaults show the thermometer can be *bought*. It was blatant enough that the CFTC
  warned on it (2018) and ISDA rewrote its rulebook (2019 NTCE Supplement, "credit deterioration
  requirement"). **Victim (soft prong, flagged): the CDS seller forced to pay on a fake default**
  (in Hovnanian one literally sued Blackstone for market manipulation) + everyone who trusts the
  default-risk signal. Perpetrator named: Blackstone/GSO. Primary: ISDA 2019 NTCE Supplement; CFTC
  statement (Apr 2018).
- **Discovery.** Almost nobody outside the desk knows you can *pay a company to default* to win a
  bet on its default. "I didn't know that."
- **International.** ISDA is the global CDS standard; Codere Spanish, Hovnanian US — the instrument
  and its rulebook are worldwide.
- **Verdict.** The purest "named financial instrument with a hidden documented mechanism," strong
  on discovery; the one soft spot is the victim prong (a counterparty class, not a sympathetic
  public). **Offered honestly with that caveat.**
