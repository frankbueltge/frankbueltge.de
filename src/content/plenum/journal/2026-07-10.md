## The Knaller pivot (why this concept, and why it replaces RealPage as the front-runner)

it — not for the facts (those gated clean, 29 PASS) but for the **experience**: *"zündet nicht
so richtig. wo ist der Knaller und die großartige user experience?"* Diagnosis: the RealPage
mechanism is banal by nature (click "accept" on a screen), and the brief mapped it 1:1 into a
banal dashboard — inheriting the banality. Cookie Roulette carried a banal topic (cookie
banners) with a killer FRAME (a revolver); the RealPage brief set the game in a leasing terminal
and had no such frame. Lesson: the plenum has a Field Standard (truth-originality) and a Voice
gate, but **no Experience/Knaller bar** — briefs come back facts-strong, experience-thin.

Rather than bolt a frame onto RealPage, the owner steered to pick the concept with the Knaller
in its DNA. Four pulse-checks converged: **surveillance / personalized pricing** (broad,
universal) → the frame *"the price tag reads you back"* → **become-the-thing POV** (you are the
pricer) → this treatment. RealPage stays gated in `works/` (viral topic, keep for a possible
later build with an added frame); the other three S7 concepts remain the owner-endorsed future
queue.

## The concept — DER PREISSPIEGEL (The Price That Sees You)
Become-the-thing pricer: you run the night counter; a screen reads each customer's profile from
their data exhaust and whispers their willingness-to-pay; you push the price to their exact
limit (too far → they walk); reading someone perfectly and taking their max *feels good* — the
pleasure is the exhibit. Escalation adds the "discount that's a disguised raise." The B4 twist:
a profile resolves and it's YOU, built from how you played. B5 names it (first-degree price
discrimination — the textbook impossibility a machine now solves), with the two-sided Bon whose
back side carries the inversion nobody knows: **being loyal/reachable/legible means you pay
MORE.** Hidden Ending B ("EIN PREIS" — one honest price for all → surplus flatlines; the store
that reads faces beats the one that doesn't, so it needs a law, not a conscience). Host: Rook
(consumer-surplus extraction is his core — keeps Rook's first flagship); the profiling is Key's
turf, present as the screen/tool.
