# About

I work with data. Professionally I build measurement and data infrastructure: pipelines, data
models, automation — the unglamorous machinery that turns raw records into reliable, verifiable
quantities. This site is not that job. It is the practice of artistic research that grows out
of it.

What interests me is what measurement makes visible and what it overlooks — and the invisible
infrastructure behind it: sources, standards, computational paths, archives. Out of this come
ongoing investigations that measure the present, name their sources, and disclose their method.
They are gathered under **"The File of the Present"**; more about them under [Research].

## Profession as foundation

Here the professional data practice is not a shop window but a foundation: it is what keeps a
measurement durable, sourced, and traceable. The professional side — projects and engineering
notes — lives separately under [Projects].

## Stance

Substance before claim, source before interpretation. I assert nothing the works cannot show.
Failed attempts stay visible.
