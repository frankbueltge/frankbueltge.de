# Research

**Measuring the present — data, code, and the infrastructures of record.**

*A data-based artistic research practice. Instruments, not visualisations.*

This is a data-based practice of artistic research. It examines the present using the same
means by which the present is measured and administered: open data sources, code, algorithmic
procedures, and technical infrastructure.

The works are built as measuring instruments, not as pictures of data. Each one asks a
verifiable question, names its sources, and discloses its method. Whether this is art is for
others to decide; here, things are measured.

## What is being investigated

The subject is how the present appears in data — and what, in that appearance, stays visible,
invisible, or contested. Four investigations are currently running:

- **The Protocol** writes the planet's session minutes every night, from twelve open,
  citable sources.
- **Half-Life** measures how quickly public attention to major events decays.
- **Parallax** measures what eight language editions of the same disputed thing each state —
  and what they leave unsaid.
- **The Policy** calculates what the market charges the present.

Their common subject is measurement itself: its sources, its gaps, its assumptions.

## Why data

Here, data is both material and subject. Material, because it presents the present in countable
form. Subject, because every measurement contains a decision: what is counted and what is not,
from which source, at which interval. Those decisions are not hidden but stated. Form follows
the truth, not the other way around — no decorative distortion.

## Why code and infrastructure

A measurement is worth no more than its reproducibility. Code makes the procedure verifiable;
infrastructure makes it durable. The investigations run as scheduled processes, record their
results under version control, and declare what they consume and depend on. What an
investigation requires — sources, compute, dependencies — is part of what it says.
Infrastructure is not a tool kept out of sight; it is part of the subject.

## Why artistic research

Because the questions are neither purely technical nor purely scientific. The point is not to
optimise a metric, but to make perceptible how measurement orders the present — and where it
reaches its limits. Research, here, means a verifiable question, a disclosed method, and a
result that is allowed to fail. Failed attempts are not removed; they are kept as notes.

## What an "investigation" is

An investigation is a running measuring instrument that meets a strict standard: real data
with open provenance; a verifiable question rather than an effect; its own infrastructure as
part of the statement; open code and open data left behind; restraint instead of technical
maximalism.

Each investigation comes with a method sheet: sources, licences, retrieval cadence,
processing, and the limits of the method. The works' text is generated deterministically — no
language model writes it. Where a model is used, for instance to extract individual claims,
the procedure is disclosed. When a source fails, this is recorded plainly rather than quietly
patched over.

## What "The File of the Present" is

The shared frame for the investigations. "File" is meant in two senses: a collection of
records, and a continuous, versioned holding. Results are not overwritten but added day by
day; the version archive is part of the work, not merely its storage. Over time, a holding
accumulates that does not comment on the present but keeps its minutes.

## What is deliberately avoided

- Decorative data art without a question.
- Claims without a source; claims about status or context.
- Language-model prose as the work's text; AI as an effect.
- Quietly patching over errors; back-dated measurements.
- Ranking victims or aestheticising suffering; with sensitive sources, the limit is named.
- Tracking on this site.
