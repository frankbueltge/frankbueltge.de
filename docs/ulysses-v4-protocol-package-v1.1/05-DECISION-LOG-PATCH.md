# Research Ecology decision-log patch — Ulysses v4 package revision 1.1

## D-ULY-01 — Project replaces generic session

- **Status:** `ACCEPTED`
- **Decision:** Ulysses works through bounded projects grounded in concrete source situations. A clock-triggered session is no longer the unit of practice.
- **Reason:** The session cadence produced activity and artefacts independently of the strength of the problem.

## D-ULY-02 — Standing delegation replaces daily approval

- **Status:** `ACCEPTED`
- **Decision:** Frank approves a durable, versioned operating mandate. Ulysses may initiate, execute, revise, archive and terminate ordinary projects inside it without project-specific human approval.
- **Reason:** Human accountability must not turn the experiment into daily manual project management.
- **Consequences:** The mandate defines budgets, tools, data classes, protected paths and escalation triggers.

## D-ULY-03 — Safe auto-land is retained

- **Status:** `ACCEPTED`
- **Decision:** The old auto-land plus site-dispatch workflow is removed, but automatic integration of validated reversible research records remains permitted through a narrower allowlisted workflow.
- **Reason:** Repository integration is useful for continuity and provenance; the error was treating it as publication by side effect.
- **Consequences:** Auto-land cannot alter protected paths, resolve escalations, create publication approval or make a project appear on the curated works surface.

## D-ULY-04 — Repository record and curated work are distinct

- **Status:** `ACCEPTED`
- **Decision:** Active projects, studies, failures, killed lines and publication candidates may exist in the public research repository without becoming curated Atelier works.
- **Reason:** Public accessibility, archival integration and artistic endorsement are different statuses.

## D-ULY-05 — Human role is constitutional and editorial, not daily operational

- **Status:** `ACCEPTED`
- **Decision:** Frank’s routine role is limited to mandate/protocol decisions, publication review, high-risk exceptions, corrections and termination powers.
- **Reason:** The project is intended to test durable machine participation, not create a daily editorial workload.

## D-ULY-06 — Curated publication remains human-only

- **Status:** `ACCEPTED`
- **Decision:** A v4 project enters the curated works surface only through a project-specific human-approved publication manifest.
- **Reason:** Publication entails authorship, rights, public claims and responsibility that may not be inferred from a green build or model judgement.

## D-ULY-07 — Project-local automation is allowed

- **Status:** `ACCEPTED`
- **Decision:** Asynchronous, event-driven or scheduled operations are permitted when attached to an active project, justified by its score and bounded by the mandate. Generic production merely because a daily or nightly schedule fired is prohibited.
- **Reason:** Autonomy requires continuity, but cadence must follow the research problem rather than substitute for it.
