# Pilot Joint Inquiry
## The Correction That Arrives Too Late

**Status:** candidate; exact source objects must be verified during reconciliation.  
**Recommended profile:** `parallel_return` with explicit cross-examination events.  
**Participants:** Meridian, Ulysses, Ensemble.  
**Public title:** provisional.

## 1. Shared problem

> What remains operative after a public claim has been corrected?

The inquiry does not treat correction as a purely informational update. It asks how first claims, their effects and their versions persist across research records, interfaces, memory, search, models, narrative and embodied experience.

## 2. Why several practices are necessary

- Meridian can investigate the measurable and source-specific persistence of a claim and correction.
- Ulysses can construct an artistic operation in which trace, iterability, non-reversibility and changed problem conditions become perceptible without reducing them to a chart.
- Ensemble can build an experiential/material situation that produces observable first effects and later correction, then return those observations as new research material.

The inquiry is invalid if this becomes a linear service chain in which Meridian supplies facts, Ensemble visualizes them and Ulysses comments.

- a source instrument;

## 4. Shared material

Minimum:

- exact original claim/version;
- exact correction/version;
- source evidence and caveats;
- known downstream/public derivative references;
- timestamps and provenance;
- a bounded set of surfaces where persistence can be observed lawfully and reproducibly.

Optional:

- search/cache snapshots;
- model responses under versioned prompts;
- public page versions;
- participant response traces from a consented prototype;
- correction-display patterns.

Do not collect personal or sensitive data merely to enrich the work.

## 5. Meridian commitment candidate

### Local question

How does the original claim remain discoverable or influential relative to its correction within a declared, reproducible set of surfaces and time boundaries?

### Mode

Audit/reanalysis plus bounded observation or experiment.

### First move

Produce a versioned `Correction Persistence Dossier` containing:

- scope and selection method;
- original and corrected claim;
- observed surfaces;
- timing and version conditions;
- evidence snapshots;
- limitations;
- negative results;
- and a clear statement of what cannot be generalized.

### Change conditions

Meridian must be willing to revise:

- what counts as persistence;
- whether visibility is an adequate proxy for effect;
- whether the correction is the same object as the claim;
- and whether an experiential observation requires a new instrument.

### Return move

Audit factual claims in Ulysses/Ensemble outputs and use their material/participant observations to decide whether its own categories or instrument need revision.

### Kill condition

Kill or narrow if no real trace beyond trivial page caching or version history can be established.

## 6. Ulysses commitment candidate

### Local question

Can a correction undo a first trace, or does it necessarily arrive as another trace that changes but cannot erase the first event?

### Mode

Aesthetic experiment or transpositional study grounded in the exact source versions.

### First move

Construct one work probe in which:

- the initial claim produces a concrete perceptual or operational consequence;
- the correction cannot simply reset the field;
- the medium carries the asymmetry;
- and the work is not replaceable by an explanatory paragraph or comparative dashboard.

Possible strategies are not preselected. The Ulysses score must derive one operation from the material and Research Foundation.

### Change conditions

Ulysses must be willing to abandon the philosophical framing if Meridian's evidence or Ensemble's material test shows that the assumed irreversibility is constructed by the chosen apparatus rather than inherent to correction.

### Return move

Revise, challenge, archive or kill the probe after exposure to Meridian's audit and Ensemble's observations.

### Kill condition

Kill if the result is primarily a visualization of claim/correction chronology, a text interface, or a pre-known Derridean/Deleuzian thesis with decoration.

## 7. Ensemble commitment candidate

### Local question

How can the unequal force of a first instruction and a later correction become an experiential situation rather than a didactic explanation?

### Mode

Material/interactive prototype or performance score.

### First move

Build one bounded prototype in which an initial rule changes participant action or spatial/visual state and a later correction cannot fully restore the prior condition.

Initial testing should use synthetic states or informed, consenting internal participants. No public participant research without an approved affected-public/consent plan.

### Change conditions

Ensemble must be willing to reject the prototype if it only dramatizes a known thesis or turns participants into instruments without meaningful formal consequence.

### Return move

Return observed behaviours, refusals, misunderstandings or material residues to Meridian and Ulysses; revise or kill once.

### Kill condition

Kill if the prototype is merely a UI demo, moral lesson or downstream design treatment of Meridian's dossier.

## 8. Sequence

```text
0. Proposal and three local commitments
1. Meridian first move: Correction Persistence Dossier
2. Ulysses and Ensemble first moves based on the same versioned material
3. Explicit Encounters linking each local object and its conditions
4. Cross-return bundle:
   - Meridian receives work/prototype claims and observations
   - Ulysses receives empirical limitations and Ensemble residues
   - Ensemble receives Meridian corrections and Ulysses challenge
5. At most one return move per practice
6. Local positions and REVIEW
7. Human decision on public exposition; local publication remains separate
```

The coordinator may run the first moves in parallel if source material is ready. It must not force synchronization that hides different rhythms.

## 10. Research quality tests

At review ask separately:

### Reciprocal dependency

Did at least one local next move materially depend on another practice's output?

### Practice change

Did any participant revise a question, instrument, form, category, method or kill decision because of the inquiry?

### Non-assimilation

Did unresolved differences remain visible without being converted into a common conclusion?

### Local quality

Would each local output still meet its own practice's standards?

### Shared exposition

Can the public trace show the relation without turning the inquiry into an org chart or success story?

These are review questions, not scores.

## 11. Possible outputs

- Meridian: correction persistence instrument/dossier and revisions.
- Ulysses: work, study, refusal or killed probe.
- Ensemble: material prototype, work, refusal or killed probe.
- The Middle: inquiry trace/exposition.
- Research Ecology: protocol findings and possible coordinator changes.

A strong result may be a changed instrument plus two killed artworks, or a compelling work plus an inconclusive empirical audit. No output configuration is privileged in advance.
