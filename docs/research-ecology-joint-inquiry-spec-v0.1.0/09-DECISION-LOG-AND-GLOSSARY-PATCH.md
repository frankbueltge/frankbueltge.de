# Decision log and glossary patch
## Proposed shared documentation changes

## D-0XX — Joint Inquiry as a temporary coordination object

- **Date:** 2026-07-19
- **Status:** `PROPOSED`
- **Decision:** Add Joint Inquiry as a shared, temporary and voluntary coordination envelope over multiple Encounters and local projects.
- **Reason:** The current Encounter model records bounded exchanges but does not by itself sustain reciprocal multi-step work. The ecology has protected local sovereignty but has not yet produced enough consequential cross-practice transformation.
- **Consequences:** Research Core and The Middle gain a Joint Inquiry record and view. Local practices retain their own projects and outputs.
- **Non-consequences:** No new practice, master agent, common ontology or permanent agenda is created.

## D-0XY — Procedural coordinator without epistemic authority

- **Date:** 2026-07-19
- **Status:** `PROPOSED`
- **Decision:** A coordinator component may validate, dispatch and enforce resource bounds but may not synthesize meaning, accept participation, write local positions, extend or publish without accepted authority.
- **Reason:** Multi-step collaboration needs operational continuity without a new central researcher.
- **Consequences:** Event-driven local dispatch is possible; participant contexts remain isolated.

## D-0XZ — Encounters remain the atomic exchange record

- **Date:** 2026-07-19
- **Status:** `PROPOSED`
- **Decision:** A Joint Inquiry groups and references Encounters but does not replace or flatten them.
- **Reason:** Offers, transfers, corrections, challenges and refusals must remain independently inspectable and attributable.

## D-0YA — One move plus one return as default profile

- **Date:** 2026-07-19
- **Status:** `EXPERIMENTAL`
- **Decision:** The first coordinator profile uses one local first move and at most one return move per participant before review.
- **Reason:** This creates reciprocal dependency while preventing expensive, self-perpetuating session loops.
- **Consequences:** Continuation requires explicit extension and new bounds.

## Constitution note

No constitutional article needs reversal. Add a clarification to the article on temporary common questions:

> Several practices may establish a time-limited Joint Inquiry around a concrete problem or shared material. Participation remains voluntary, local methods and archives remain sovereign, and the shared inquiry does not acquire authority to synthesize participant positions. Encounters remain the inspectable records of movement between practices.

## Glossary additions

### Joint Inquiry / Gemeinsames Forschungsprojekt

A temporary, voluntary and resource-bounded coordination envelope in which two or more sovereign practices work on one concrete problem while retaining local questions, methods, outputs, claims and publication decisions. It groups Encounters and local project references but does not become a practice.

### Temporary Research Constellation / temporäres Forschungsgefüge

A conceptual description of the relations temporarily assembled by a Joint Inquiry. Not necessarily a technical object or public name.

### Local Commitment

A versioned statement issued by one participating practice describing its local question, intended operation, admitted materials, conditions, resource bounds, return obligation, escalation policy and local publication policy.

### Local Move

A bounded advance in one participant's local project that produces a report and zero or more local object references relevant to the inquiry. A move is an operational unit, not a quality judgment.

### Return Move

A participant's bounded response after exposure to another participant's admitted output. It may revise, challenge, transform, refuse or leave the first move unchanged.

### Coordinator Component

A technical actor that validates, dispatches, correlates and enforces limits for a Joint Inquiry. It has no authority to determine meaning, research quality or publication.

### Season

An optional future grouping of multiple Joint Inquiries. A Season must remain voluntary and temporary and is not part of the v0.1 implementation.
